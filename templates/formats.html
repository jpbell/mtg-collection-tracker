MTG Format Rules

Review construction guidelines and gameplay parameters for the most popular formats.

Commander / EDH

Commander
Deck Size Exactly 100 Cards
Copies Per Card Singleton (Max 1)
Starting Life Total 40 Life
Legality Pool Eternal (All Sets)

🔮 Core Format Mechanics

  • The Commander: Choose a Legendary Creature (or specific eligible Planeswalkers) to lead your deck. The rest of the deck is placed in the Library, while your Commander begins the game in the separate Command Zone.
  • Color Identity: Every card in your deck must only feature colors that appear on your Commander (including mana symbols in card text). Colorless cards can always be added.
  • Commander Cast & Tax: You can cast your Commander from the Command Zone for its standard mana cost. Each subsequent time you cast it from the Command Zone, it costs an additional {2} generic mana (known as the "Commander Tax").
  • Commander Damage Rule: If a player is dealt 21 or more points of combat damage by a single Commander over the course of the game, that player loses the game immediately, regardless of their current life total.
  • Multiplayer Focus: Commander is typically played in casual free-for-all games of 4 players.

Standard

Standard
Deck Size 60+ Cards (Sideboard: up to 15)
Copies Per Card Max 4 copies (Lands: unlimited)
Starting Life Total 20 Life
Legality Pool Recent Rotated Sets (~3 Years)

🔮 Core Format Mechanics

  • Rotational Set Pool: Standard is a dynamic format that only permits cards printed in release sets from the last three years. Older sets rotate out of legality every autumn as new sets enter the pool.
  • Construction Guidelines: A minimum of 60 cards is required for the main deck. You may optionally build a 15-card sideboard to swap cards in between games during a match (typically best-of-three matches).
  • Playstyle: Standard is primarily a 1-on-1 competitive format, characterized by shifts in the metagame as card rotations happen.

Modern

Modern
Deck Size 60+ Cards (Sideboard: up to 15)
Copies Per Card Max 4 copies (Lands: unlimited)
Starting Life Total 20 Life
Legality Pool 8th Edition (2003) to Present

🔮 Core Format Mechanics

  • Non-Rotational Set Pool: Modern is a non-rotating format, meaning cards do not leave legality unless banned. It includes all cards from 8th Edition (the introduction of modern card frames in 2003) up to the most recent sets, plus specific Modern-direct expansion sets (e.g. Modern Horizons).
  • Match Rules: Built for competitive 1-on-1 gameplay. Main decks must have at least 60 cards, with optional 15-card sideboards.
  • Banlist Managed: Due to the high power level of cards printed since 2003, Wizards of the Coast actively manages a banlist to keep competitive gameplay balanced and interactive.

Pioneer

Pioneer
Deck Size 60+ Cards (Sideboard: up to 15)
Copies Per Card Max 4 copies (Lands: unlimited)
Starting Life Total 20 Life
Legality Pool Return to Ravnica (2012) to Present

🔮 Core Format Mechanics

  • Bridge Between Standard & Modern: Pioneer is a non-rotating format designed to allow players to use cards that have rotated out of Standard but are not powerful enough to compete in Modern.
  • Card Legality: All standard releases from Return to Ravnica (Autumn 2012) to the present day are legal. Direct-to-Modern sets (like Modern Horizons) are NOT legal in Pioneer.
  • No Fetch Lands: To differentiate its gameplay and keep deck prices lower, all five original fetch lands (e.g. Polluted Delta, Flooded Strand) are banned in Pioneer.

Legacy

Legacy
Deck Size 60+ Cards (Sideboard: up to 15)
Copies Per Card Max 4 copies (Lands: unlimited)
Starting Life Total 20 Life
Legality Pool Eternal (All Sets)

🔮 Core Format Mechanics

  • High Power Eternal Format: Legacy is an eternal format that allows cards from every set in MTG's history (including Commander products, supplemental draft sets, and promotional cards).
  • Strict Banlist: While all sets are legal, exceptionally powerful cards (such as the Power Nine, Black Lotus, and fast mana artifacts) are banned entirely to prevent instant-win combinations.
  • Strategic Complexity: Features highly complex stacks and interactions, utilizing cards like Brainstorm, Force of Will, and Wasteland to establish a deeply tactical metagame.

Pauper

Pauper
Deck Size 60+ Cards (Sideboard: up to 15)
Copies Per Card Max 4 copies (Lands: unlimited)
Starting Life Total 20 Life
Legality Pool Common Rarity Cards Only

🔮 Core Format Mechanics

  • Commons-Only Legality: Pauper is a popular non-rotating format where every single card in your main deck and sideboard must have been printed at the Common rarity in at least one paper or digital Magic release.
  • Budget Competitive: Because only commons are permitted, Pauper is extremely budget-friendly while retaining an exceptionally high power level comparable to Legacy in terms of spell efficiency (e.g., Lightning Bolt, Counterspell, and Ponder are legal).

Vintage

Vintage
Deck Size 60+ Cards (Sideboard: up to 15)
Copies Per Card Max 4 copies (Restricted: Max 1)
Starting Life Total 20 Life
Legality Pool Eternal (No Bans, only Restrictions)

🔮 Core Format Mechanics

  • The Highest Power Eternal Format: Vintage permits cards from all sets throughout MTG history. Unlike Legacy, cards are almost never banned for power level reasons.
  • Restricted List: Instead of banning cards, Vintage features a "Restricted List". Highly powerful cards like Black Lotus, Ancestral Recall, Moxen, and Sol Ring are restricted to exactly one copy per deck.
  • Fast-Paced Games: Games can sometimes be decided on the very first turn. Vintage allows players to experience the absolute peak of Magic's historical power level.
